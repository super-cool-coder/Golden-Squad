Studying can be a hard process. However, there are many tips to help you study better and to make studying easier for you - this page shows how!

Golden Revision Tips

Revision is very important, as it makes you review what you have learnt. You could make notes to prepare for exams. A way to master revision is to recap each subject covered that day for about 15-20 minutes. Additionally, while preparing for the test, you can make sample questions for each topic with random levels of difficulty (this works the best with Science, RE, History and English). Another awesome tip that some of the Golden Squad uses ourselves is: the Blurt Tip! This means to read and revise your textbooks, a little at a time, then cover the revision notes and write down everything you remember. If you missed out something, write it down on your paper with a different coloured pen. Also, highlight the key points in your textbook and/or revision notes. Why don't you have a fun mock quiz with friends and family? And finally, read everything but write it in your own words so you understand the concept.

Stuff & Supplies - To Help you Revise!

Many tools and stationary need to be used to be a good noter - not to mention all the revision books you need. The best stationary to always keep with you are:

  • A highlighter - To extract key info
  • Coloured pens - To highlight the buzz words
  • A black biro - To write
  • A pencil, eraser and sharpener - To draw and label diagrams
  • A ruler - To measure
  • A calculator - To solve high-level maths problems and science/li>
  • A gluestick - Just in case any important worksheets are given to you

Good book companies include: CGP, Pearson Edexcel and Collins. The website BBC Bitesize is also very well-suited for revision purposes.
